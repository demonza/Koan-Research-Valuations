Methodology, data hygiene & honest limitations

The model. Three-stage owner-earnings DCF per share: an explicit stage at your growth rate, an optional fade stage where growth glides linearly to g∞ (set fade to 0 for the classic two-stage), and a terminal value by Gordon growth or exit multiple. Share-count drift converts company FCF into FCF per share, so buybacks accrete and dilution costs you — the quantity Buffett actually talks about.

The point estimate is the least important number on this page. The sensitivity grid, scenarios and Monte Carlo exist because the honest output of a DCF is a range. If the range comfortably straddles the price, the correct action is usually nothing.

Reverse DCF is the honest lens. The strip solves for the stage-1 growth the current price already assumes, holding your r and g∞ fixed. If that implied growth looks easy for the business, the market is cautious; if it looks heroic, the market is optimistic. Audit the price — don't manufacture one.

Data hygiene (why the freshness pill still exists). Prices refresh live via the server (↻ button, 10-minute cache). But prices were never the real staleness risk — fundamental anchors are: base FCF, shares outstanding and net cash only update when you read the filing. The pill tracks price age; the quarterly routine is unchanged: after each earnings report, refresh FCF / shares / net cash from the filing. Fundamentals are FY2025 reported figures unless noted. Quotes come from an unofficial public endpoint — if it ever breaks, the workstation degrades gracefully to manual prices.

Known distortions in the anchors. SPGI's base FCF carries a provisional ~13% trim for the Mobility spin (completed 2026-07-01) — overwrite it with the recast figures published 2026-07-06. Novo, AENA and Saab have capex-depressed or lumpy FCF; normalise before trusting the output. Nestlé's FY2025 FCF was flattered by a one-off distribution.

Not covered by DCF: VUAA (S&P 500 UCITS ETF) and VWCE (FTSE All-World UCITS ETF) are diversified baskets — value them by aggregate market multiples, not a single-entity DCF. Xetra-Gold generates no cash flow, so a DCF is undefined; gold is priced on scarcity and monetary demand.
Portuguese CGT is 28% flat — intrinsic-value gaps only matter after tax and only if you'd actually transact; low turnover stays structurally correct.
